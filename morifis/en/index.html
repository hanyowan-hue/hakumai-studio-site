How to play

Open the forest. Then protect it.

Spend mana to open the closed forest and carve out paths. Protect the Komorebi Stone and send every intruder back to the woods. The rules are simple — but where you open changes everything.

01

Open the forest

It all starts with a closed forest. Spend mana to open it, and the cleared ground becomes the path your enemies will walk. Open rich soil, and the forest's spirits — the Ibuki — wake to join you.

02

Hide the Komorebi Stone

Place the Komorebi Stone somewhere along the paths you've opened. The farther it sits from the entrance, the better hidden it is — harder for enemies to find, and easier for you to defend.

03

Fight them off

Enemies slip in from the entrance, hunting the stone. Meet them with Flowers, Roots, and the Ibuki you've woken — send them all back to the forest before the stone is carried away, and the day is yours.

The game

Gentle, unhurried.
A strategy of guarding the forest.

Open the forest with mana, and keep the Komorebi Stone safe. Step in anytime in real time, yet never feel rushed — think it through one move at a time.

Wake the Ibuki Awaken

Forest friends

The forest wakes, friend by friend.

Open rich soil and the forest's spirits — the Ibuki — wake up. Every Ibuki you meet is recorded in your encyclopedia, and they change form as they grow. Here are just a few.

Moss ibuki

Mokoke

First to wake from the rich soil — a soft, mossy little one, busily carrying nutrients around.

Winged ibuki

Hotari

Drifting lightly over the opened paths. The smallest wings in the forest.

Keeper of the sunlight

Leafin

A little beast clad in leaves. The more it grows, the brighter its green.

Keeper of the waterside

Hasunoko

Perched alone on a lily pad, watching over the dewdrop springs.

Moonlit mushroom

Akari

Soft lights blinking on in the night woods — a moonlit signpost.

Snow spirit

Yukipon

A drop of ice dancing in the Snowveil Forest, dressed in glittering crystals.
